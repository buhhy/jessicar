New User Experience

As of March 2016, Quora had 100 million monthly unique visitors to the product. With this amount of growth, it is important for the company to convert more visitors into happy, retained users.

New NUX

At Quora, I was able to touch on many parts of the product. I was drawn to improving the New User Experience (NUX), especially due to the user criticism. I felt it was not receiving as much attention as it deserved. I started with running many small experiments to understand the space as best I could. Eventually, I fully assumed the responsibility to improve NUX.

Add Knows About Topics

When users sign up, they want to read great content right away. Some users felt that Quora was putting unnecessary work between them and the content. However at this point in time, Quora needed to get explicit signal on what users liked to read. Therefore, we learned that we needed to keep the step, for now.
I explored the problem further by conducting research via user interviews, workshops, and brainstorms. Although we had to keep the step, it did not mean that our NUX had to be as painful as it was for our users. The research boiled down to two main problems.

Problem 1

Users perceived the product as untrustworthy due to the low design quality and product transparency.

Problem 2

Users felt forced to work in order to see content, without an understanding of why they should sign up.

I took the problems and transformed them into goals and principles to inform the redesign and experiments to come.

Trust ++

The new experience should present itself as a carefully crafted and transparent product (e.g., beautifully show the value).

Simplicity ++

The new experience should reduce cognitive load to help the user get past it quickly and easily (e.g., presenting fewer options, simple words).

Our qualitative goals had to be measured using metrics and user feedback. I worked with the project manager to determine which metrics to use as a proxy for measuring success. We anticipate a big gain in brand, which is often an improvement that takes time to take effect.
I designed a new concept to achieve a more trustworthy and simple NUX.

Clearer Design & Navigation

Users now have a clearer understanding of the signup process

Fewer Choices

Users have to pick from only a few topics at signup

Simpler wording

Users can parse short and concise language faster and more easily
